🎨 National Museum of Fine Arts

📍 Padre Burgos Ave, Ermita, Manila
🏛️ Home to iconic Filipino masterpieces including Juan Luna's "Spoliarium" and Félix Hidalgo's "Las Virgenes Cristianas Expuestas al Populacho". This neoclassical building houses the largest collection of Philippine art from the 17th to 20th centuries.

🦕 National Museum of Natural History

📍 Agrifina Circle, Ermita, Manila
🌿 Featuring the "Tree of Life" elevator at its center, this museum showcases the rich biodiversity of the Philippines. Highlights include the complete skeleton of Lolong (the largest crocodile in captivity), preserved flora and fauna, and fascinating geological exhibits about the country's natural wonders.

🏺 National Museum of Anthropology

📍 Agrifina Circle, Ermita, Manila
📜 Journey through the archaeological and ethnographic heritage of the Philippines. See the "Maitum Jars" (anthropomorphic burial jars from 5 BC), ancient goldwork, traditional indigenous textiles, and the fascinating "Maritime and Underwater Cultural Heritage" gallery. A deep dive into 50,000+ years of Filipino history.

🏮 Binondo, Chinatown Manila

📍 Manila, Metro Manila (Oldest Chinatown in the world)
🥢 Established in 1594, Binondo is the world's OLDEST Chinatown! A vibrant district where Chinese-Filipino culture thrives. Walk through Ongpin Street, visit the historic Binondo Church (Minor Basilica of St. Lorenzo Ruiz), and indulge in authentic Chinese cuisine. From hopia & tikoy to fresh lumpia and dumplings — this is a food lover's paradise with rich history at every corner.
